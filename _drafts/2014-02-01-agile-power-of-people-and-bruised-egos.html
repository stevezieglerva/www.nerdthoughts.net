This week, I was reminded of how Agile democratizes teams. I'm helping on a corporate initiative around Agile and we took a vote on which tool to use.  I thought to myself "What a great idea!" After I voted, I realized just about everyone else voted on a different tool and I thought to myself "What about MY idea???" It was enlightening exercise to see the opinions of my peers. It ended up being a great exercise because I got to learn a new tool and I saw how experts use it in a real context.

It also got me thinking about how we're trying Lean Coffee on several of our team meetings. I clearly remember how one of my awesome, obviously important topics, only got one vote: mine. It was clear the team wanted to discuss other items that we're more important to them.

Tough lessons for a former Command and Control PM, but all for the better of the team and project.
