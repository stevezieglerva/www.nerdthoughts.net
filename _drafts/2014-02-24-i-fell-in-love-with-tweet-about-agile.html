A user story is a conversation starter. A requirement is a conversation killer.
— Sandy Mamoli (@smamol) February 22, 2014
I absolutely love this tweet:

• It's a great summary of Agile philosophies. Constant conversations about needs, priorities, and quality are essential to delivering software that meets needs.
• It reminds me of Humphrey's Law that users really don't know what they want until they see working software (maybe not even then).
• I hate the word "requirement." It has such an authoritative and definitive connotation.  It assumes that you can completely understand what you want well before any software is built. It also assumes every need has equal priority and equal value. I have a running joke every time I hear the story of a large Federal IT project that fails. I like to find out how many requirements they had or how many pages the specification document was. I proclaim that if only they had doubled or tripled the requirements or pages, then the project would have been a success!  :)

Sandy's not sure where she heard it, but I'm glad she shared it.
