Ever since I had my first mobile device (Palm Pilot), I've been searching for a good, cheap, shopping list app. Besides maintaining a list of items, the app has to do some other critical things:


  • Upload a pre-existing list from text or CSV-  I already had an electronic version of a large list and wasn't about to tap all of it into the app
  • Sync for desktop access - It's often a lot faster to update data through the desktop.  I needed an easy way for bulk updates, either adding new data or checking lots of items for a new trip
  • Fast - The removal of items during the shopping trip should be fast.  It was hard to believe that many apps did not do this. One of the apps had an animation of the app icon unchecking the item each time! For a long time, I used Microsoft's OneNote and had a simple checkbox list note that worked really well ... until they upgraded it and made it slower with lots of screen jank. One app prevented use while it sync'd after each item update.
  • Order the list by store aisle with custom aisle names - I need to be able to sort the list by the order of the aisles in the store and I want to be able name the aisle/category myself.
So, I finally settled on the OurGroceries for iOS that met all of the critical things above. They do all of them well and have a very fast in-store user experience. There's still a few things that I think could be improved like allowing separate categories per store.  I also noticed early on that the companion website was not the prettiest or most modern. But, the Agilest in me reminded me that pretty and modern weren't part of my critical needs.  I'm sure they appropriately focused on the app experience over the website experience. 



I'm still working towards the nirvana of having my wife use it also with a shared, synchronized list. But, I don't think OurGroceries provides change management consulting.
