I still haven't found a great way to bookmark Twitter links for future reading.  I've found effective ways to favorite, email, or put them into a Google doc. The breakdown is usually in the finding time to actually read them. I ran into this again recently when Kin Lane posted two items (on the same day) whose titles easily grabbed my attention: Cutting Back On My Traveling And Speaking and Clarification On The Cease And Desist I Got From @Pluralsight.  I'm usually checking Twitter in short bursts of dead time which is not ideal for reading posts.

But, I do find when I open up Feedly, my RSS reader, it is at a time when I can read a full post and appreciate it. I added him to my reader and was pleasantly surprised at seeing some of the headline/images (stuff I totally missed from just following him on Twitter).



I saw several posts with images of the Veterans Administration (VA) seal that caught my eye. It turns out that Kin was a Presidential Innovation Fellow with the VA. He has some interesting and honest posts about frustrations with government bureaucracy. It's tough to read that an agency with obvious issues can't consistently and easily use technology and bright minds to help our vets.

So, if you can't keep up with the Twitter firehose, at least switch the noozle to mist by adding people you like and respect to your reader.
