Elasticsearch has some great ways to provide a fast, customized autocomplete experience for users. There are many ways to accomplish it which can lead to confusion for a beginner researching the topic. The approaches generally include using prefix queries, edge ngrams, or suggesters with appropriate tokenization and filtering. The approach you take depends on the user experience you're trying to provide and the scale/performance you need. Here are some great posts describing different approaches.


  • Search like a Google with Elasticsearch. Autocomplete, Did you mean and search for items. by Volodymyr Bilyachat. It's a very descriptive post on how to use shingles to create multi-word autocomplete suggestions sorted by their frequency of use. It also describes how to use Did You Mean for spelling corrections.
  • Multi-field Partial Word Autocomplete in Elasticsearch Using nGrams by Sloan Ahrens. He enable partial completions on multiple words and introduced me to a creative use for selective field inclusion in the _all field.
From Sloan's post

  • Building Scalable Search from Scratch with ElasticSearch by Ram Viswanadha. This is a good post describing the scalable architecture and use of Angular.js. 
From Ram's post


  • You Complete Me on the Elastic Search blog. It's a good description of the Completion Suggester which is designed for speed and scale.
