--- layout: post title: My Custom OneNote Ribbon date: '2013-07-01T00:48:00.001-04:00' author: Stephen Ziegler tags: - OneNote - productivity modified_time: '2013-07-01T00:50:38.791-04:00' thumbnail: http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lM0ZXJ-U_qA/UdEJBwS-GxI/AAAAAAAAgqU/NA438VX8kJQ/s72-c/OneNote+Toolbar.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1727955271917225446.post-7418975006504742796 blogger_orig_url: http://www.nerdthoughts.net/2013/06/my-custom-onenote-ribbon.html --- One of my New Years resolutions for 2012 was to stop taking paper notes.  Little did I know that it would be so easy with Microsoft's OneNote 2010.  I had started playing around with EverNote, but stopped when I heard Scott Hanselman mention he liked the free flowing content placement better.  Another big selling point was Microsoft's OneNote iOS app.

My goal was to be able to do more than type live notes. I'm an extreme visual thinker so I needed to be able to draw and annotate images in real-time also. I quickly realized that I needed a custom ribbon combining my most-used items from the Home, Insert, and Draw ribbons.

So, here is my customized ribbon. It highlights the:

  • Select and Type
  • Custom Tags
  • Insert Space
  • Screen Clipping
  • Drawing
  • Table
  • and Zoom items
