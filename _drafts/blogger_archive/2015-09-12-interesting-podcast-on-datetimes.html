US Timezones by Rosie Rosenberger



It's hard to believe that Datetimes can be interesting, but Matt Johnson led a really good discussion on the issues in dealing with dates and times in computers on the Hanselminutes podcast. Dealing with time precisely and effectively is even more important in this era of global systems. It's made really hard by the fact that days aren't always 24 hours and that timezones and daylight savings rules are constantly changing.

Matt mentioned the havoc that moving servers to different timezones can cause. He recommends always setting servers to GMT and localizing the time on the client side. It brought back some bad design decisions I made decades ago in trying to synchronize dates in an enterprise client/server application. Hopefully that code has been re-factored!
