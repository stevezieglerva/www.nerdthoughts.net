--- layout: post title: Why I Love Kanban - Instant Fit To Your Processes date: '2013-01-05T14:47:00.001-05:00' author: Stephen Ziegler tags: - kanban - agile modified_time: '2013-01-19T00:45:22.634-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1727955271917225446.post-4770747575922852843 blogger_orig_url: http://www.nerdthoughts.net/2013/01/why-i-love-kanban-instant-fit-to-your.html --- I am a huge fan of Kanban.  One of the biggest reasons is that applying Kanban to your current processes is not hard or disruptive.  You just visualize what you are currently doing.  As the folks at Crisp say:

In Kanban you start with whatever process you have, visualize it, introduce WIP limits, and then evolve from there.


 Awesome visual from Jeff Patton

Your visualization can be as simple as a board with To Do, Doing, and Done lanes. The most disruptive part is introducing the Work In Progress (WIP) limits.  I know this is blasphemous to say, but I think you can hold off on introducing WIP for a while.  Just the visualization and continuous improvement will pay off for an organization used to waterfall. 

It's best summarized in a quote that I read on Twitter.  It's killing me that I can't find it now and attribute the credit but here is what I remember:
You don't do Kanban.  You apply Kanban to your current processes.
